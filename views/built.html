The lightouse crew used to work in three-days shifts, rotating between the total of 23 lightouses which were co-founded by Norway, Iceland and Faeroers. The crews sailed in groups between the islands, either windsurfing in summer, or on yachts during the winter gales. This ensured a good balance between physical effort and a rather sedentary work of a lighthouse operator.
